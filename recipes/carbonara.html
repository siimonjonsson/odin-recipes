Pasta Carbonara

Description

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Italian dish that originated in Rome. It is made with simple ingredients, including pasta, eggs, cheese, pancetta or bacon, and black pepper. The dish is known for its creamy texture and rich flavor, which comes from the combination of eggs and cheese. The pancetta or bacon adds a savory and slightly salty taste to the dish, while the black pepper provides a subtle kick. Pasta Carbonara is a quick and easy meal that can be prepared in under 30 minutes, making it a popular choice for weeknight dinners or when you want a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces spaghetti
  • 4 ounces pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the pancetta or bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove from the skillet and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess fat.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated Parmesan cheese until well combined. Set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.
  5. Return the cooked pancetta or bacon to the skillet and stir to combine with the garlic.
  6. Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet and toss to combine with the pancetta and garlic.
  7. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing vigorously to coat the spaghetti evenly. The residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little bit of the reserved pasta water to thin it out to your desired consistency.
  8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Be cautious with the salt, as the pancetta and Parmesan cheese are already salty.
  9. Serve immediately, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.
